The International Business Association Leadership

Allison Yee

Allison Yee is a senior at San Jose State University. She is pursuing a major in International Business and a minor in Spanish. Last fall, she studied abroad in Barcelona, Spain at Toulouse Business School. Her time living and traveling abroad enhanced her understanding of other cultures, and she looks forward to interacting with others who share her interests.

Allison has always been fascinated by foreign languages and cultures, and she often spends her free time self-studying a language that interests her. She has a passion for traveling and hopes to one day explore Japan, South Korea, and Turkey. In addition to her passion for traveling and interest in other cultures, Allison is a puzzle enthusiast. She aims to be a globally-aware leader working towards innovative solutions to meet global needs.

Our Team

Samaki Dorsey
Officer

Samaki Dorsey is a Bay Area native and Junior at San Jose State University student studying International Business with a French minor. The field of Corporate Social Responsibility is her career focus. Samaki's goal is to work on local and globally impactful projects that support the intersection between a company and its accountability to the environment, employees and community. She is passionate about doing work that is impactful and improves the health and well-being of the public. Heavily active in her own community, Samaki volunteers with City Slicker Farms, a local non-profit committed to food justice and urban farming.

In her spare time, Samaki loves to travel. She has visited Japan, Italy, Brussels, U.K., Spain and traveled across much of the United States. Her next adventure is a study abroad in southern France. She is a Karaoke enthusiast and occasionally performs in professional musical theater. Other hobbies include gardening, cooking, cycling and listening to NPR or French radio. Akin to her talent for growing vegetables in her backyard garden, she excels as a self-described “cross pollinator” connecting ideas and solutions, people with people, and developing lasting relationships.

Khanh Nguyen
Officer

Khanh Nguyen is a second year student at San Jose State University who is pursuing a degree in International Business. He plans to minor in either Japanese or French. Born in San Jose, Khanh hopes to one day travel the world, experiencing new cultures and traditions as well as taking a bit of each country with him.

Khanh believes that the way to grow mentally and spiritually is through travel. If we stay in one place for our entire lives, all we know is that one place, but by traveling and going on journeys, people can grow to become more well rounded global citizens. One of Khanh's interests is learning languages.Khanh is currently trying to learn French and Japanese on his own time and he hopes to master Mongolian, Mandarin and Korean before he turns 30. Other than that, his interests include walking, exploring new places, bird watching, reading, and drinking coffee.

Lucas Salles Freitas
Officer

Lucas Salles Freitas is an international student from Rio de Janeiro, Brazil and a senior at San Jose State's Lucas College of Business. He is pursuing a B.S. in International Business and a double-minor in Spanish and Global Leadership/Innovation. Last year, he attended Toulouse Business School in Barcelona, Spain. His passions include: traveling, movies, coffee, soccer, economics and comedy. He joined the International Business Association to promote San Jose State's cultural diversity, to encourage students in going abroad, and to help International Students feel welcome in our campus. After graduating, he aims to attain a graduate degree and to work with international trade and development.
